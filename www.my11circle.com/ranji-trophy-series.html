Ranji Trophy 2019-2020

India’s first-class domestic cricket tournament is set to take over the cricketing community from December 2019 to March 2020. Don’t miss out on this cricket craze. Stay updated on Ranji Trophy teams, schedule, players’ performance, news, and lots more only on My11Circle.

What is Ranji Trophy?

If cricketers want to play for India at the international level, then this is the place to showcase their talents, tenacity, and perseverance. Ranji Trophy is the most prestigious domestic championship in India. Every year state, regional, and government teams fight it out for four months to be crowned the ultimate champs. The matches are held on neutral grounds to have a mixed crowd cheering the team. The BCCI intricately plans the tournament right to the Ranji Trophy finals.

These 38 talented teams are divided into four groups:

  • Elite Groups A and B: This is the top tier with nine team and only five teams will qualify.

  • Elite Group C: This is the second tier that has ten teams and just two teams will move to the quarter finals.

  • Plate Group: It is the lower tier, which has the latest teams and one squad will enter the next round. They can have three professional players in a team.

The Format for the Tournament:

There are two formats followed in the Ranji Trophy tournament:

  1. Round-robin format: In the Ranji Trophy, this format is followed in the initial stages of the tournament. What happens is that the teams in the groups will play each other. These matches span over four days.

  2. Knockout format: This format comes in for the qualifying stage. The top teams of each group fight it out to enter the semi-finals and the losing teams are knocked out from the tournament. In this format, the matches stretch for five days.

INTERESTING SCOOP ON RANJI TROPHY

  • BCCI launched the Ranji Trophy in 1934 as The Cricket Championship of India. 

  • The first match was between Madras (Chennai) and Mysore at Chepauk on November 4.

  • The tournament gets its name from Prince Ranjitsinhji, who was one of the first Indian test cricketers. He played for England and Sussex. He introduced the late cut, leg glance, and back-foot defense in cricket.

  • Maharaja Bhupinder Singh of Patiala present the trophy as a gift to the tournament.

  • Did you know, Vijay Hazare, Wasim Jaffer, Faiz Fazal are some of the talent discovered at the Ranji Trophy? 

  • Before 2016-17, the matches were held on the grounds of either one of the teams.

  • Some of the stadiums are Atal Bihari Vajpayee Stadium, Vidarbha Cricket Association Stadium, Goa Cricket Association Academy Ground, Vidarbha Cricket Association Ground, etc.

  • In 2019, BCCI confirmed the use of limited Digital Review System (DRS) for the knockout matches; it excludes the use of use Hawk-Eye and UltraEdge.
