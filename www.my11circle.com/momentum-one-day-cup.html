Momentum One Day Cup 2020

What is the Momentum Cup?

In South Africa, every year six teams from across the country compete in the Momentum One Day Cup. It is one of South Africa’s biggest domestic cricket competitions. One day matches are held to determine the winner. The Momentum One Day Cup 2020 will be the 39th edition and it will be held from February to March.

Format of Momentum ODI

The Momentum One Day Cup follows the List A format. It is a limited-overs or one day type of cricket matches. Most of them are day-night matches with 50 overs match.

Hollywoodbets Dolphins

The team was founded in 2003 and represents the KwaZulu-Natal province. Some of the well-known cricketers that were part of the Dolphins were players Shaun Pollock, Imraan Khan, Jonty Rhodes, Andrew Hudson, and many more. Presently, the team’s coach is Grant Morgan and the captain is Khaya Zondo.

Imperial Lions

This squad represents Central Gauteng and North West. In 2003, the team was founded when the Central Gauteng Lions merged with North West. Today, the team is guided by their captain, Temba Bavuma and trained by the coach, Wandile Gwavu (Interim).

Momentum Multiply Titans

The team was founded in 2004, when Eastern Cricket Union united with Northerns Cricket Union. They are the defending champions and one of the top teams in the country. The team’s coach is Mark Boucher and the captain is Grant Thomson.

VKB Knights

The team was earlier known as Diamond Eagles. VKB Knights consists of Free State and Griqualand West first class cricket areas in the country. They are coached by Nicky Boje and the captain is Pite van Biljon.

Warriors

The team was set up in 2003, and it is the union of Eastern Province and Border first class cricket teams. Warriors represent Port Elizabeth and Eastern Cape Province. Their captain is the talented JJ Smuts and their coach is Robin Peterson.

Cape Cobras

As the name suggests, the team represents Cape Town and Western Cape. The team was formed in 2005, and they are one of the most prominent teams. Their present captain is Dane Piedt and Cape Cobras are trained by Ashwell Prince.

History of Momentum Cricket

In 1981-82, a tournament was launched Benson and Hedges Series, which had five teams. Over the years, more teams were included to the series. By 1994-95, there were 11 teams. However, in 2004–05 the United Cricket Board of South Africa revamped the tournaments calling it the Momentum One Day Cup. It had six franchise teams competing with each other.
